A bee brain has less than 1 million neurons. Humans have ~86 billion. 
\Yet bees can still tell apart from flowers, hive matter, spatial patterns, and even human faces! 
Yes. surprisingly, honey bees can recognize human faces. I don't want to mislead you by stating that 
recognizing faces as something that bees naturally care about. In the wild, this could not be a surprising outcome. 
However, experiments done in labs have trained bees to associate a specific human face image with a sugar reward. After training, the bees could, 
1. Pick the correct face from several options, 2. Generalize across slightly altered versions of the same face. Isn't that interesting?
