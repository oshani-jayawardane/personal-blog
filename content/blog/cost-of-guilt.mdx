## The Weight We Were Never Meant to Carry

Dear Reader,

I have been thinking. Would you rather sacrifice happiness for success, or success for happiness? Is that truly a tradeoff, or was it never meant to be one? What stops us from being both happy and successful at the same time? Some believe that happiness is negatively correlated with the energy we put into a task. The world is full of advice, each defining happiness in its own way. But what if I find joy in the very effort itself in doing what satisfies me? What if the work that demands my energy is also what fulfills me? Would that not lead me to success? Probably yes.

Then what is stopping us? It is guilt. The invisible weight of expectations and responsibilities that chains us to the ground and makes us feel undeserving of reaching for the stars. It is the hard work that goes unrecognized, and the time that feels wasted on futile labor. In the end, it is not the effort that drains us, but the guilt of defying the boundaries we believe we must obey. Such guilt is a liability that can be written off with self-gratitude.
